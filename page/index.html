Science
Galaxy formation Background: Galaxy formation in the epoch of reionization and beyond How and when the first galaxies form in the earliest Universe is one of the most fundamental questions in modern astronomy. The pristine materials in the early universe cooled down to form neutral hydrogen at redshift z ~ 1000 or 380k years after the Big Bang. Once first stars and galaxies are born, however, the hydrogen gas in the intergalactic space is ʻreionizedʼ by hydrogen-ionizing photons from the hot, massive stars.
